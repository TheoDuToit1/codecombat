# Gauntlet Mode Summary (Reference)

## Core Structure
- **Run Structure:**
  - Players draft a deck (or select a team/characters) and play a set number of matches (e.g., 4 in Duelyst II), regardless of win/loss.
  - Emphasis on variety and accessibility—everyone gets to play the same number of games per run.

## Drafting/Selection
- **Buckets System:**
  - Instead of picking one card at a time, players choose from "buckets" (groups) of cards, increasing variety and synergy potential.
  - After drafting, players can remove some buckets to refine their deck/team.
- **Weights:**
  - Some cards/characters appear more often than others, allowing for balance and archetype diversity.
- **Cross-Faction/Type:**
  - Occasionally, options from outside the chosen faction/type are offered for more variety.

## Gameplay Mechanics
- **Enemy Logic:**
  - Enemies are spawned in waves or as part of the level design.
  - Enemy types are varied, but each type is simple (sometimes just a single sprite, no animation, for performance).
  - Movement is smooth and can be 8-directional, not grid-based.
  - Many enemies can be spawned at once, so memory/performance is a priority.
- **Player Logic:**
  - Player movement is also smooth and not grid-based for consistency.

## Rewards & Progression
- **Rewards:**
  - Players earn rewards based on performance, but the system is designed to be generous and accessible.
  - Random rewards (cards, gold, etc.) are common, with some scaling for more wins.
- **Progression:**
  - Runs are short and repeatable, encouraging experimentation and replayability.

## Design Philosophy
- Focus on fun, variety, and accessibility.
- Encourage trying new strategies and combinations.
- Avoid high-stakes, high-pressure environments—runs are meant to be quick and enjoyable.
